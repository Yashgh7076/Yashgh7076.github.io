Speech Processing Projects

Speech Enhancement using Non-linear Estimate of Statistical Properties of Non-stationary Noise Sources

Contributors: Remy Kabel, Amritpal Singh Gill

Our project focused on designing and building a single-channel speech enhancement system for far-end noise reduction. The given speech signal was assumed to be corrupted with additive complex Gaussian noise.

Project Challenges:

  • Estimating power spectral density (PSD) of both non-stationary noise and clean speech
  • Developing an adaptive algorithm for changing statistical properties of noise
  • Keeping algorithm complexity low for potential use in digital hearing aids

Algorithm for Speech Enhancement:

Flowchart of algorithm for adaptive speech enhancement

Key Components of the Algorithm:

  1. Dividing speech into segments
  2. Smoothing each segment
  3. Estimation algorithms:
    • Decision Directed (DD) approach for SNR estimation
    • Non-linear MMSE based estimation of noise PSD
  4. Undoing segmentation

Results:

We tested the algorithm at different input SNR levels. The enhanced speech signals showed significant improvement in clarity and perceptibility, especially at low SNR values.
