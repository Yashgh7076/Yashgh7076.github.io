About me

Hi, this is Yadnyesh Luktuke! I'm actively looking for a job in the domains of data science, computer vision, machine learning and signal processing. Up until recently I was a graduate student in Clemson University, where my research focused on automatic segmentation and classification of eating related activities from wrist-motion data using deep learning. As a part of this research, we developed a novel neural network model that was capable of simultaneously detecting and labeling periods of eating activities from wrist-motion data that was recorded when subjects were eating an unscripted meal.

I also have experience in predictive analysis and designing classification models for data such as census records, images, speech and stock-prices using techniques from computer vision, pattern recognition, machine learning and signal processing. Most of my programming experience lies in using languages such as Matlab, Python and C. However I am comfortable learning new programming languages and technologies, and am keen to expand my horizons in other related domains as well. In addition to this, I am a skilled instructor, having taught undergraduate and graduate students and have overseen their end of semester projects.

Educational background

I received my Bachelor's in Electronics and Telecommunication Engineering (2012) from Maharashtra Institute of Technology - Pune, which was affiliated with the University of Pune. I then obtained my Master of Science in Electrical Engineering (2017) from Delft University of Technology (TU Delft), The Netherlands. At TU Delft my research focused on intensity normalization of 3D brain MRI images of the same patient using machine learning. This approach is thought to be useful in automatic diagnosis and follow-up studies of patients, especially those that relocate after serious medical conditions or injuries. I will soon graduate from Clemson University, USA with a Master of Science in Computer Engineering in May 2020.
