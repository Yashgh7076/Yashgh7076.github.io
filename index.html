About me

Hi! I'm Yadnyesh Luktuke, an engineer who is actively looking for a job in the domains of data science and machine learning. Up until recently I was a graduate student in Clemson University. My research focused on automatic segmentation and classification of eating related activities from wrist-motion data using deep learning. I have previously worked on predictive analysis of different types of data, such as census records, images, and speech using techniques from computer vision, machine learning, pattern recognition and signal processing. I am skilled at data analysis and system programming using Python and C. I also have experience teaching undergradute and graduate students in areas from electrical, electronics and computer engineering.

Educational Background

I received my Bachelor's in Electronics and Telecommunication Engineering (2012) from Maharashtra Institute of Technology - Pune, which was then affiliated with the University of Pune. I then obtained my Master of Science in Electrical Engineering from Delft University of Technology (TU Delft), The Netherlands in 2017. My research at TU Delft focused on intensity normalization of 3D MRI scans of the brain using machine learning. I will soon graduate from Clemson University, USA with a Master of Science in Computer Engineering in May 2020.
