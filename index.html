Recent work

At Clemson University I conducted research on deep learning models for simultaneously detecting and classifying periods of wrist motion activity into pre-defined categories known as eating gestures. These are useful to analyze eating behavior in humans, and also for the development of an automatic energy intake monitoring system such as the original Bite Counter developed at Clemson University. To the best of our knowledge this is the first work in the world that has successfully developed a deep learning model for performing semantic segmentation on 1D time-series data recorded using inertial measurement unit (IMU) sensors. Please see the Deep learning page on this website for more information. Our work has been submitted for review to the `2020 IEEE International Conference on Big Data'.
