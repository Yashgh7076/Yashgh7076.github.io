Deep Learning Projects

Segmentation of eating gestures

Advisor: Dr. Adam Hoover

Link to thesis: Segmentation and recognition of eating gestures from wrist motion using deep learning

We developed a novel neural network capable of simultaneously detecting and classifying periods of wrist-motion data into different categories related to eating gestures. Our model achieved an accuracy of 80% with a standard deviation of 20% on average per meal.

A portion of a meal after being marked by trained raters. The gestures are indicated using, red: bite, aqua: drink, orange: utensiling, black: rest and gray: other.

Deep Learning Model

Our pipeline uses a deep learning model with a stack of convolutional blocks followed by a stack of deconvolutional blocks. This architecture allows for effective mapping of input data to feature space and back to the required output space.

Comparing ground truth (top) against classifier output (bottom) using the same color labels as mentioned above.

Model Evaluation

We evaluated our model using various metrics, including the percentage of correctly identified indices and gestures per meal. The results showed high accuracy and consistency across different meals and gesture categories.

Histogram of percentage accuracy in each meal against the total number of meals with that particular percentage.

Future Work

Our future work includes designing a model that can identify gestures from associated wrist-motion in the instrumented hand that occurs due to actual wrist-motion in the uninstrumented hand. We also aim to expand the total number of gesture categories and investigate deeper neural network architectures.
